Introduction

Most of the government funded universities have no tuition fees and students only have to bother about living expenses (ranging from 8 to 9 lakhs per year) in terms of financial obligation.

Admission for fall begins by Feb and end by April or so. Last date of submission of application highly depends on University

There are two mediums of language namely German and English for master’s program in Germany. The application requirement and the selection criteria is highly subjective to a particular department in the particular university. So it is very important to check the program requirement in the desired university

In Germany, very few universities and programs require GRE. But those who do are majorly concerned with your score in QUANTITATIVE. If GRE is a requirement, make sure to score above 160 at the least. A score above 165 is considered good and those up to 168 and above gives 90 to 95% chance of clearing admission requirement. For Verbal, the score above 150 or 155 is appreciable (but do verify once from admission requirements page)

It is recommended to learn up to A2 level before going to Germany, as it is a requirement for part-time jobs offered around university. The advantages and disadvantages of both options are listed below.

English

Here the entire course of study is in English. The level of German required for application is usually “None” (but also subjective to university, so verify once). However, once you get an admit, the visa process requires a level up to A1 or A2. This is to make sure that you communicate well during your stay. Advantages

    Advantages

  • i. Minimum level of German proficiency is A1 or A2 depending on university.
  • ii. The curriculum is easy to comprehend and fewer chances of failing exams.
  • iii. Lesser expense and hassle in learning higher and tougher German level

    • Disadvantages

  • i. Limits the number of universities one can apply to, multi fold. If there are 100 universities in Germany related to your interest, only 12 to 20 will offer courses in English, which obviously increases the competition exponentially.
  • ii. After graduation, one may face challenges in landing job opportunities if they are not accustomed to German level up to B2 or C1. Knowing up to C1 is important for landing a good job, and many say it can’t be done without.

German

Here the program is taught in German Language. No English whatsoever is used and the application process demands certificate up to B2 or C1 level for admission.

    Advantages

  • i. One can apply to all the universities there are in Germany. So 90 to 95% chances in getting into the desired university.
  • ii. Ease in finding part-time job opportunities, internships, full time job etc

    • Disadvantages

  • i. The course is rigorous and many students fail to comprehend the German level even after having C1 certificates.
  • ii. Chances are there that you fail exams or even semesters due to this.

Resume building

For students solely interested in cracking interviews and entering the job market in Germany, it is essential to understand what the interviewers “EXPECT” during the hiring process. Following information is subjective, and drafted through personal experience from people either interning or having a full time job, after completing their master’s in Germany

1. WORK EXPERIENCE is important

Statistically speaking, if you don’t have a full time work experience (that is pursue MS directly after completing UG), the chances of even getting your job application notices reduces by 50 to 60%.

Even after getting picked and called for an interview, you’ll be competing against someone who has 2 to 3 years of job relevant experience either in their home country or Germany. You WILL be compared against them, and chances of being selected further drops by 60%.

Hence, work experience IS important.

2. DEPTH of KNOWLEDGE is important

The job interviews in Germany are more of a thorough discussion over your resume and question answer session on the topics of your project, in contrast to back to back question asked on any topic.

Thus it is necessary to have in depth knowledge of topics on all the projects, research work and internships you mention in your resume. They will first hear you out about what all you have done so far, and then dissect the material on a microscopic level. After testing your fundamental knowledge on the subjects you yourself presented to them, they may slightly change the problem statement. It will be obviously be in the same domain, but slightly changed, and will ask you to solve it based on skills you have. This is to test your applicative skills of solving problems based on knowledge you acquired during UG and MS.

WHY IS ALL THIS IMPORTANT FOR SOMEONE STILL IN THEIR UG? Good question.

Knowing this gives a sense of awareness of what to do. Also now you consciously walk the extra mile while doing any internships or projects, which are the building block of your resume

Things to do during Internships

A. TALK

Yeah! It is very important to talk to people during internships. More or less everyone lands an internship in UG through personal contacts and favors, and they limit their reach only in one department under one person.

If it is a manufacturing industry, roam around the plant. Explore all departments of the firm. The material procurement folks, the processing guys, packing and distribution people, RnD department, design team, senior management level, and anyone else working there. Achieve a total sense of the company site. Note it and report it.

If it is service industry comprising IT, finance etc, chart the overall work flow of the firm. How responsibilities are distributed within the company, what skills are expected from them. This will provide insight of what more this is to learn in terms of language, DS Algo and ML Ai. It also makes you realize what salary you’ll get after doing all that, the lifestyle and whether you want to settle for that or not.

This highlights your dedication of learning and the vigilance of being curious during the term you were with them.

B. Score a project

In India, vocational training is falsely labeled as “internship” and sold in campus interviews. The difference between the two lies in “applicative work” in the latter. Internship actually means working hours and completion of set targets. This is unfortunately not even offered in vocational training students do in their summer and winter breaks of UG.

So its important you score some project, some work, some responsibility from your mentor during your internship. It doesn’t matter how small it is or how relevant it is, What matters is that you have something to say when someone asks, “what did you do in this internship”. The answer should be “I solved this..” or “I worked on this….” Instead of “I learnt this…”. No one is interested in what you learnt, but what did you do with “what you learnt is a charm to hear.

GRE and IELTS

GRE preparation timeline is highly subjective. Still here’s a brief dissection of the preparation process. It can be cleared in 4 months or even less if studied with dedication. Some people may even require 6 or more months depending on their retaining capacity.

There are ONLY two parts. English. Mathematics.

Paper Pattern:-

  • Writing (1hour)
  • Verbal Reasoning (30 minutes)
  • Quantitative Reasoning (35 minutes)
  • Verbal Reasoning (30 minutes)
  • Quantitative Reasoning (35 minutes)
  • Verbal/Quantitative (30/35 minutes)

Out of the last 5 sections, 4 are fixed. Remaining one can be either Quantitative or Verbal and this is not scored. However,there is no way of knowing whether the 2nd or 3rd of 4th or 5th or 6th section is the special one. In the personal experience of many candidates, it will be either extremely easy or extremely difficult, but this is not always true. So it is important to approach each section with EQUAL vigor and attention.
